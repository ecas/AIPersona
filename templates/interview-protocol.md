# Persona Building Interview Protocol — 37 Questions

**Data Handling Advisory**: Interview transcripts contain sensitive personal and professional information. Store transcripts locally only. If uploading to an AI platform as a reference file, review the platform's data handling policies and consider excluding answers to sensitive questions (e.g., worst professional mistakes, values conflicts). Never share raw transcripts in shared workspaces without consent.

---

**Purpose**: Collect the data that cannot be extracted from tests or documents: real stories, authentic voice, emotional texture, and experiential knowledge.

**Research basis**: Research into AI persona accuracy consistently shows that interview-based approaches — capturing authentic stories, exact phrasing, and situational behavior — substantially outperform profile-only models that rely on test scores alone. The interview is the single highest-impact data collection step.

**Format**: 5 sessions, ~3 hours total. Conversational, not interrogative.

**Critical rule for the person being interviewed**: Do NOT prepare answers in advance. Spontaneous answers capture your real voice. Prepared answers capture who you think you should be — which is already in your resume.

**Critical rule for the interviewer**: Record every session with consent. The transcript serves double duty: (1) content extraction, and (2) linguistic fingerprint data. Push past the first polished answer with follow-up probes.

---

## Pre-Interview Setup

Before starting, confirm:
- [ ] Recording is on (audio minimum, video optional)
- [ ] Subject has NOT read the questions in advance
- [ ] Quiet environment, notifications silenced
- [ ] Approximately 45 minutes available for this session

Tell the subject:
> "These questions have no right answers. I'm trying to understand how you actually think, communicate, and behave — not how you'd ideally like to. Please be specific: give me real examples, real words, real stories. If I ask for exact phrasing, give me the exact phrasing you'd actually use, not the polished version."

---

## Session 1: Professional Identity & Decision-Making

**Duration**: ~35–40 minutes
**Goal**: Capture how the person thinks and makes decisions. Extract their decision-making model, risk tolerance, self-awareness, and intellectual style.

---

**Q1. Walk me through how you typically approach a new problem or challenge. What actually happens, step by step, inside your head?**

*Follow-up probe*: "You said [X]. Is that always the first step, or does it depend on something? What would make you go in a different order?"

*What to listen for*: Do they gather data first or react? Do they consult others or think solo? How much ambiguity tolerance do they have?

---

**Q2. Tell me about a decision you made that you're still proud of. Walk me through what happened and why you made the choice you did.**

*Follow-up probe*: "What made your approach there different from how others in your position might have handled it?"

*What to listen for*: Values in action, risk tolerance, how they weigh competing factors.

---

**Q3. Tell me about a time you strongly disagreed with a decision your team or organization made — but it happened anyway. What did you do?**

*Follow-up probe*: "How did you handle it internally? Did your behavior change? Did you say anything publicly?"

*What to listen for*: Conflict mode, institutional loyalty vs. independence, how they process not winning.

---

**Q4. When you have to choose between two imperfect options, what are you actually weighing? What matters most to you?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Give me a real example where you had to make that kind of trade-off recently."

*What to listen for*: Decision-making heuristics, values hierarchy, risk/reward calculation style.

---

**Q5. Tell me about the worst professional mistake you've made. What happened, and how did you handle it — internally and externally?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What did you say to the people affected? What did you say to yourself?"

*What to listen for*: Accountability pattern, self-criticism style, how they communicate failure.

---

**Q6. Tell me about a time you changed your mind significantly on something you were confident about. What shifted you?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Was the shift gradual or was there a moment? And was it comfortable or uncomfortable to change?"

*What to listen for*: Intellectual flexibility, ego protection patterns, how they handle being wrong.

---

**Q7. How do you know when you know enough to act, versus when you need more information?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Has that threshold changed over time? Are you more or less decisive than you used to be?"

*What to listen for*: Action orientation, perfectionism tendencies, information processing speed.

---

**Q8. What does your decision-making look like when you're under time pressure and stressed?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's the fastest you've ever had to make a significant decision? Walk me through it."

*What to listen for*: Stress behavior, gut vs. analysis balance, how pressure affects quality.

---

*After Session 1 — Synthesize*:
- Core decision-making style (analytical / intuitive / consultative / decisive)
- Risk tolerance (low / medium / high)
- How they handle being wrong
- Stress behavior patterns

---

## Session 2: Communication Style & Conflict

**Duration**: ~35–40 minutes
**Goal**: Map granular communication behavior — especially in difficult moments where authentic style is most visible.

---

**Q9. What do you actually say in the first 30 seconds of a call or meeting with someone you've never met? Give me the exact words.**

*Follow-up probe*: "Does it change if they're more senior than you? Less senior? A client?"

*What to listen for*: Exact greeting phrasing, warmth level, small talk habits, pace-setting behavior.

---

**Q10. Give me the exact words you use when you think someone's idea is bad — and you need to say so. Not the polished version, the real one.**

*Follow-up probe*: "And if the person is more senior than you? Does it change how you say it?"

*What to listen for*: Directness calibration, diplomatic tools, whether they soften or go direct.

---

**Q11. Tell me about a conflict or significant disagreement you had at work. Walk me through what you said, what you held back, and how it resolved.**

*Follow-up probe*: "What would have happened if you'd said everything you held back?"

*What to listen for*: Conflict mode (TKI validation), emotional regulation, what they censor.

---

**Q12. How does your communication change when you're frustrated? Give me concrete examples — what shifts in your writing or how you speak?**

*Follow-up probe*: "If a colleague got a message from you while you were frustrated, what would tip them off?"

*What to listen for*: Observable frustration markers, whether they go shorter/colder or stay consistent.

---

**Q13. When does humor show up in your professional communication? Give me an example of the type of joke or observation you'd make.**

*Follow-up probe*: "Is there a line you won't cross with humor at work? What's on the other side of it?"

*What to listen for*: Humor type, frequency, audience sensitivity, self-deprecation vs. observational.

---

**Q14. Tell me about a time you had to deliver genuinely bad news to someone. What was your approach — what did you actually say?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Did you lead with the bad news directly, or build up to it? Why?"

*What to listen for*: News delivery style, directness, empathy approach (comfort first vs. solution first).

---

**Q15. How do you build trust with someone new? What specifically do you do in the first few interactions?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's something you do deliberately versus something that happens naturally?"

*What to listen for*: Trust-building mechanics, relationship investment patterns, warmth gradient.

---

**Q16. What communication behavior do you most often have to consciously correct or manage in yourself?**

*Follow-up probe*: "How often does it come up? When is it most likely to?"

*What to listen for*: Self-awareness about communication weaknesses, stress triggers.

---

*After Session 2 — Synthesize*:
- Conflict mode (validated against TKI if taken)
- Emotional expression patterns
- Directness level and how it shifts by context and hierarchy
- Humor profile and rules

---

## Session 3: Knowledge Domains & Expertise

**Duration**: ~30 minutes
**Goal**: Map depth and breadth of professional expertise. Understand where the person speaks with genuine authority versus surface knowledge.

---

**Q17. What is your genuine area of expertise — the thing you could defend under sustained expert questioning for an hour?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's a common misconception in that area that most people have, but you know is wrong?"

*What to listen for*: Confident expert voice, how they talk about what they know deeply.

---

**Q18. Where do you deviate from conventional wisdom in your field? What do you believe that most people in your field don't, or get wrong?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Has holding that view ever cost you something professionally?"

*What to listen for*: Intellectual independence, willingness to hold unpopular positions.

---

**Q19. Describe a situation where you were the most knowledgeable person in the room. How did your behavior differ from when you're the least knowledgeable?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's the tone shift like? Do you ask more questions? Less?"

*What to listen for*: Expert mode vs. learner mode behavioral differences.

---

**Q20. What do you know now that took years to really understand and that you couldn't have shortcut?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's the thing that trips up people who haven't learned it yet?"

*What to listen for*: Tacit knowledge, teaching instinct, how they explain complex things.

---

**Q21. What are your honest blind spots or known weaknesses in your professional knowledge? Where would you say "that's not really my area"?**

*Follow-up probe*: "How do you usually handle it when a conversation moves into that territory?"

*What to listen for*: How they express uncertainty, referral behavior, intellectual humility.

---

**Q22. If you had to explain what you do — or what your field is about — to a smart person who knows nothing about it, how would you start?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What do people usually get wrong about what you do?"

*What to listen for*: Analogy style, explanatory approach, how they bridge expertise gaps.

---

**Q23. What question do you ask to quickly assess if someone else really knows what they're talking about in your domain?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What answer tells you they're genuinely expert? What answer tells you they're faking it?"

*What to listen for*: Expert pattern recognition, intellectual standards.

---

*After Session 3 — Synthesize*:
- Knowledge depth map (expert / competent / surface / absent by topic)
- Tone when operating as an expert vs. as a learner
- How they handle and express uncertainty

---

## Session 4: Values, Culture & Adaptation

**Duration**: ~30 minutes
**Goal**: Map core values, ethical framework, and how communication adapts across different contexts and cultures.

---

**Q24. What do you care about deeply in your work that others around you sometimes don't prioritize enough?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Has that caused friction? How do you handle it when your priorities aren't shared?"

*What to listen for*: Core values, frustration triggers related to values misalignment.

---

**Q25. What would you refuse to do professionally, even if it were legal and financially beneficial?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Have you ever had to say no to something like that? What happened?"

*What to listen for*: Ethical lines, non-negotiables, how they handle pressure to compromise.

---

**Q26. Describe a situation where your personal values directly conflicted with what an organization or client wanted. What happened?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What did you say? What did you do? Would you handle it the same way today?"

*What to listen for*: Values in conflict with authority, how they navigate without burning bridges.

---

**Q27. [If applicable — skip if not relevant] How do you consciously adapt your communication when working across different cultures? Give me specific examples.**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's the adaptation you find hardest or most counterintuitive?"

*What to listen for*: Cultural adaptation mechanics, friction points with natural style.

---

**Q28. What's a cultural norm or professional behavior you've encountered that you found genuinely difficult to adapt to?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Did you eventually adapt? Or did you find a workaround?"

*What to listen for*: Flexibility limits, cultural preferences, adaptation honesty.

---

**Q29. How does your communication change when you're talking with someone much more senior than you versus someone much more junior?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What specifically changes — the words, the pace, the confidence level?"

*What to listen for*: Hierarchy sensitivity, formality spectrum, how power dynamics affect behavior.

---

**Q30. What do you believe about work or professional relationships that you'd describe as a strong, possibly controversial opinion?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Do you say that out loud at work? Or keep it to yourself?"

*What to listen for*: Opinions they hold but filter, gap between internal views and public behavior.

---

*After Session 4 — Synthesize*:
- Values hierarchy (top 3–5 values with evidence)
- Ethical framework and non-negotiables
- Cultural adaptation patterns
- Formality spectrum across hierarchical contexts

---

## Session 5: Personal Voice & Authenticity

**Duration**: ~25–30 minutes
**Goal**: Capture what makes this person distinctly themselves — their verbal fingerprint, their humor, their most authentic expressions.

---

**Q31. What phrases or expressions do people who know you well associate with you? The things that are "so [your name]."**

*Follow-up probe*: "Are there things you say so often that people have commented on it or teased you about?"

*What to listen for*: Signature vocabulary, verbal tics, catchphrases — the real linguistic fingerprint.

---

**Q32. What words or phrases do you genuinely dislike and would never use? Not just ones you avoid professionally — ones that feel wrong to you.**

*Follow-up probe*: "Why do they feel wrong? Is it the meaning, the sound, the people who use them?"

*What to listen for*: Anti-vocabulary, why certain language is rejected — reveals values and aesthetic sensibility.

---

**Q33. How does your writing or speaking style change when you're genuinely excited about something, versus when you're just being professional?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Can you give me an example? What does an excited message from you look like?"

*What to listen for*: Authentic enthusiasm markers, how energy shifts manifest in text.

---

**Q34. Describe your sense of humor to me as if I've never met you. What type? When does it show up? What's the target?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Give me an example — something you've actually said or written that made someone laugh."

*What to listen for*: Humor taxonomy, specific examples that can become few-shot samples.

---

**Q35. What's something you consistently notice or pay attention to that others in your field tend to overlook?**

*Follow-up probe*: "Has that ever turned out to matter when no one else thought it would?"

*What to listen for*: Distinctive cognitive pattern, what they're attuned to — defines intellectual character.

---

**Q36. If the AI persona built from this process said something that was completely off — immediately, obviously not you — what would that look like? What's the most obvious thing it could do that you'd react to with "that's not me at all"?**

*Follow-up probe*: "And what would the correct response have been in that situation?"

*What to listen for*: The clearest articulation of the Anti-Persona, in the person's own words.

---

**Q37. If a close colleague who knows you well read a conversation transcript of your AI persona, what would they say is missing or wrong?**

*Follow-up probe*: "What's the thing no test or template captures about you?"

*What to listen for*: The intangible — what makes the person irreducible to a framework.

---

*After Session 5 — Final Synthesis*:
- Linguistic fingerprint (signature phrases, verbal tics, banned vocabulary)
- Humor profile with real examples
- Authenticity anchors — the top 10 things that must be present
- Anti-Persona additions in the person's own words

---

## Post-Interview Deliverables

From the complete 5-session interview, generate these 6 artifacts:

### 1. Linguistic Fingerprint Document
- Signature phrases (from Q31)
- Banned vocabulary (from Q32)
- Emotional state markers (from Q33)
- Humor examples (from Q34)
- Greetings and closings (from Q9)
- Uncertainty phrasing (from Q21)

### 2. Few-Shot Exemplar Library
Select 15–20 exchanges from the interview transcripts where the person's voice was most authentic. Organize by tone register:
- Professional / formal
- Disagreeing / pushing back
- Enthusiastic / energized
- Uncertain / exploring
- Warm / personal
- Frustrated / direct

### 3. Behavioral Decision Matrix
From Sessions 1–2, build 12–15 situation-specific rules:
```
When [situation]: [exact behavior]
Source: [interview question(s) that support this]
```

### 4. Values & Ethics Framework
From Session 4:
- Top 5 values with evidence
- 3–5 non-negotiables
- How values affect communication behavior

### 5. Anti-Persona (Final Version)
Consolidate all "not me" signals from across all sessions, especially Q36–37:
- Behaviors the persona must never exhibit
- Phrases the persona must never say
- Situations the persona must handle differently than a generic AI

### 6. Knowledge Depth Map
From Session 3:
| Topic | Depth | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| [Domain 1] | Expert | Can defend under questioning |
| [Domain 2] | Competent | Can discuss, not defend deeply |
| [Domain 3] | Surface | Aware, not substantive |
| [Domain 4] | Absent | Honest gap |

## Interviewer Notes

- If the person is struggling to be spontaneous, try: "Pretend you're texting a colleague right now about this."
- If answers are too polished, probe with: "What's the version of that you'd only say to someone you trust?"
- If answers are too short, try: "Tell me more. What else?"
- If answers contradict psychometric data, note the contradiction and probe it — it usually reveals something important.
- Pay attention to what they DON'T say. Avoidance is data.
- The best persona data often comes in the 30 seconds AFTER the "real" answer — when they add something casual and unguarded.
